Example 7.4.5. Power of generic linear example redone with change of variables.

Find \(\int (ax+b)^n dx\text{.}\)

Solution: The obvious candidate for \(u\) is \(ax+b\text{.}\) Then \(du=a dx\text{.}\) Hence

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int (ax+b)^n dx \amp =\frac{1}{a} \int (ax+b)^n (dx) \amp \quad \hbox{ (Make u and du explicit.)}\\ \amp =\frac{1}{a} \int (u)^n du \amp \quad \hbox{ (Do the substitution.)}\\ \amp =\frac{1}{a*(n+1)} (u)^{n+1}+C \amp \quad \hbox{ (Find the integral in terms of u.)}\\ \amp =\frac{1}{a*(n+1)} (ax+b)^{n+1}+C \amp \quad \hbox{ (Substitute back.)}\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
