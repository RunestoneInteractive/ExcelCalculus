If I have a dollar in the bank at an effective annual interest rate of 6%, compounded continuously, at what rate is the principal increasing after to years,

Solution: We know \(f(x)=(1.06)^x\text{.}\) Thus \(f'(x)=\ln(1.06)(1.06)^x\text{.}\) We evaluate this at 10 years and get \(f'(10)=\ln(1.06)(1.06)^{10}=.10435074\text{.}\) Thus after 10 years, I am earning a little more than 10 cents a year.
