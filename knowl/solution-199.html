Solution 6.2.1.1.1.

  1. Give the 2 functions of one variable through \(P_1\) obtained by holding each variable constant.

    \begin{equation*} f(4,y)=16+12y+4y^2 \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} f(x,2)=x^2+6x+16 \end{equation*}

  2. Find the partial derivatives of the original function.

    \begin{equation*} f_x (x,y)=2x+3y \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} f_y (x,y)=3x+8y \end{equation*}

  3. Evaluate the partial derivatives at \(P_1\text{.}\)

    \begin{equation*} f_x (4,2)=8+6=14 \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} f_y (4,2)=12+16=28 \end{equation*}

  4. Give the equation of the tangent plane through \(P_1\text{.}\)

    We need \(f(4,2)=16+24+16=56\) for the equation of the tangent plane.

    \begin{equation*} Linear\ f(x ,y)=f_x (x_0,y_0 )(x-x_0 )+f_y (x_0,y_0 )(y-y_0 )+f(x_0,y_0 ). \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} Linear\ f(x,y)=14(x-4)+28(y-2)+56 \end{equation*}

  5. The approximation at \(P_2\) obtained from the tangent plane.

    \begin{equation*} Linear\ f(3,2.5)=14(3-4)+28(2.5-2)+56 \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} = -14+14+56=56 \end{equation*}
