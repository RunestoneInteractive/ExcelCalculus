Example 7.4.1. Power of a linear by guess and check.

Find \(\int (3x+5)^7 dx\text{.}\)

Solution: We could do this problem by rewriting the integrand as an explicit seventh degree polynomial and then using the power and sum rules, but that is too much work. Instead I will notice the integrand looks almost like a power, and thus guess an answer of \(\frac{1}{8} (3x+5)^8+C\text{.}\) I then check by differentiating. Using the chain rule

\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dx} (\frac{1}{8} (3x+5)^8+C)=\frac{1}{8}*8(3x+5)^{8-1}*3=3(3x+5)^7. \end{equation*}
.

Thus our guess was off by a factor of 3 and the correct antiderivative is

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{3}*1\frac{1}{8} (3x+5)^8+C=\frac{1}{24} (3x+5)^8+C. \end{equation*}
