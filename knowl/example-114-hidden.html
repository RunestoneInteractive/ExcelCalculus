Find the integral \(\int \sqrt{2x}+\sqrt[3]{4x}\ dx\text{.}\)

Solution

\begin{equation*} \int \sqrt{2x}+\sqrt[3]{4x}\ dx =\int \sqrt{2x}\ dx+\int \sqrt[3]{4x}\ dx\qquad\hbox{(sum rule)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\sqrt{2}\int \sqrt{x}\ dx+\sqrt[3]{4}\int \sqrt[3]{x}\ dx \qquad\hbox{(constant multiple rule)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\sqrt{2}\int x^{(1/2)}\ dx+\sqrt[3]{4}\int x^{(1/3)}\ dx\qquad\hbox{(rules of exponents)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\sqrt{2} x^{(3/2)}(2/3)+\sqrt[3]{4}x^{(4/3)}(3/4)+C\qquad\hbox{(power rule)} \end{equation*}
