Solution 5.2.17.1.

Take the derivative with respect to \(t\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dt} 960=5 \frac{d}{dt}[L^{0.25} K^{0.75}] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 0=5[\frac{d}{dt} (L{0.25}) K^{0.75}+L^{0.25} \frac{d}{dt} (K^{0.75}) ] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 0=[0.25 L^{-0.75} \frac{dL}{dt} K^{0.75}+L^{0.25} 0.75K^(-0.25) \frac{dK}{dt}] \end{equation*}

Solve for \(\frac{dL}{dt}\)

\begin{equation*} 0.25 L^{-0.75} \frac{dL}{dt} K^{0.75}=-L^{0.25} 0.75K^(-0.25) \frac{dK}{dt} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \frac{dL}{dt}=\frac{-L^{0.25} 0.75K^(-0.25)}{ 0.25 L^{-0.75} K^{0.75}} \frac{dK}{dt} =-3 \frac{L}{K} \frac{dK}{dt} \end{equation*}

If \(L=81\text{,}\) \(K=256\text{,}\) \(\frac{dK}{dt}=5\text{,}\) then we have \(\frac{dL}{dt}=-3 \frac{81}{256}5 \approx -4.75\text{.}\)
