Example 7.4.6. Power of quadratic function with change of variables.

Find \(\int (2x^3+11)^7 x^2 dx\text{.}\)

Solution: The obvious candidate for \(u\) is \(2x^3+11\text{.}\) Then \(du=6x^2d\)x. Thus

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int (2x^3+11)^7 x^2 dx \amp =\frac{1}{6} \int (2x^3+11)^7 6x^2 dx) \amp \quad \hbox{ (Make u and du explicit.)}\\ \amp =\frac{1}{6} \int (u)^7 du \amp \quad \hbox{ (Do the substitution.)}\\ \amp =\frac{1}{6*8} (u)^8+C \amp \quad \hbox{ (Find the integral in terms of u.)}\\ \amp =\frac{1}{48} (2x^3+11)^8+C \amp \quad \hbox{ (Substitute back.)}\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
