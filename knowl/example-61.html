Example 4.3.3. Successive processes.

We have two processes that need to be run in succession to produce gizmos. The yields of the two processes are given by:

\begin{equation*} intermediate(raw)=-25+7.3 raw+.2 raw^2 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} gizmos(intermediate)=-5+12 intermediate \end{equation*}

Find the rate of production in terms of the amount of raw material.

Solution: We want to find the derivatives of the individual processes and then use the chain rule.

\begin{equation*} \frac{d\ intermediate}{d\ raw}=7.3 +.4 raw \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \frac{d\ gizmos}{d\ intermediate}=12 \end{equation*}

Thus

\begin{equation*} \frac{d\ gizmos}{d\ raw}=\frac{d\ gizmos}{d\ intermediate} \frac{d\ intermediate}{d\ raw}=12*(7.3 +.4 raw) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =87.6+4.8 raw \end{equation*}

The rate of production is a linear function of the amount of raw material used.
