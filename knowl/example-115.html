Example 7.3.3. Antiderivative of power and exponential functions.

Find the integral \(\int 2*3^5+4e^x\ dx\text{.}\)

Solution

\begin{equation*} \int 2*3^5+4e^x\ dx=\int 2*3^5\ dx+\int 4e^x\ dx\qquad\hbox{(sum rule)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =2\int 3^5\ dx+4\int e^x\ dx\qquad\hbox{(constant multiple rule)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\frac{2}{\ln(3)} 3^5+4 e^x+C\qquad\hbox{(exponential rules)} \end{equation*}
