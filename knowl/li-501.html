Item b.

We can write \(g(q)\) as \(f(h(q))\) where \(h(q)=q^2+6\) and \(f(q)=\sqrt{q}\text{.}\) We use the rules from the previous section to compute \(h'(q)=2 q\) and \(f'(q)= 1/(2\sqrt{q})\text{.}\) Composing we get \(f'(h(q))= 1/(2\sqrt{(q^2+6 )})\text{.}\) Thus

\begin{equation*} g'(q)=f'(h(q)) h'(q)=(2 q)/(2\sqrt{(q^2+6 )}). \end{equation*}
