Example 6.3.6. Finding and Classifying Critical Points.

Let \(f(x,y)=x^3-3x+y^3-3y^2\text{.}\) Find the critical points and classify them using the discriminant.

Solution: We start by computing the first partial derivatives.

\begin{equation*} f_x=3x^2-3=3(x-1)(x+1) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f_y=3y^2-6y=3(y-2)(y). \end{equation*}

Then we compute the second partial derivatives and the discriminant.

\begin{equation*} f_{xx}=6x,\quad f_{xy}=0,\quad f_{yy}=6y-6,\quad D=(6x)(6y-6)-0^2=36xy-36x. \end{equation*}

We have critical points when both first partials are 0, so at \((1,2)\text{,}\) \((-1,2)\text{,}\) \((1,0)\text{,}\) and \((-1,0)\text{.}\)

At \((1,2)\text{,}\) both \(D\) and \(f_{xx}\) are positive, so we have a local minimum.

At \((-1,2)\) and \((1,0)\text{,}\) \(D\) is negative, so we have a saddle point.

At (\((-1,0)\text{,}\) \(D\) is positive and \(f_{xx}\) is negative, so we have a local maximum.
