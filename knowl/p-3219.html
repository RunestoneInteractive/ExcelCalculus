Solution: As we did in example 2, our first guess uses the basic rule without worrying about the linear term, so we guess \(e^{ax+b}+C\). We then take the derivative of that expression and obtain \(ae^{ax+b}\). This misses our integrand by a factor of \(a\). We adjust by that factor and find the antiderivative is \(\frac{1}{a} e^{ax+b}+C\).
