Solution: As we did in example 1, we first guess the antiderivative to be \(\frac{1}{n+1} (ax+b)^{n+1}+C\text{.}\) We then take the derivative of that expression and obtain \(a(ax+b)^n\text{.}\) This misses our integrand by a factor of \(a\text{.}\) We adjust by that factor and find the antiderivative is \(\frac{1}{a} \frac{1}{n+1} (ax+b)^{n+1}+C\text{.}\)
