We thus find it very useful to be able to systematically find an anti-derivative of a function. The standard notation is to use an integral sign without the limits of integration to denote the general anti-derivative. Thus \(\int_a^b f(t)dt\) is referred to as the definite integral of \(f(x)\) from \(a\) to \(b\text{,}\) and is a number. In contrast, \(\int f(x) dx\) is the indefinite integral of \(f(x)\) and it is a function. We use indefinite integrals or anti-derivatives to evaluate definite integrals or areas.
