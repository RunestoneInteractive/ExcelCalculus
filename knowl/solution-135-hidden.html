Let \(h(x)=\sqrt{5x^7+3x-2}=u^{1/2}\text{,}\) with \(u= 5x^7+3x-2\)

\begin{equation*} h'(x)=1/2 u^{-1/2} du=1/2 (5x^7+3x-2)^{-1/2} (35x^6+3) \end{equation*}

Another way to think of the chain rule is that we need to take derivatives of the different functions that make up the composite function:

\begin{equation*} h(x)=\sqrt{5x^7+3x-2}=(5x^7+3x-2)^{1/2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} h'(x)=\text{"derivative outside function"}*\text{"derivative inside function"} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =[1/2 (5x^7+3x-2)^{-1/2} ][(35x^6+3)] \end{equation*}
