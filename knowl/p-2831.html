Solution: As we did in example 2, our first guess uses the basic rule without worrying about the linear term, so we guess \(e^{ax+b}+C\text{.}\) We then take the derivative of that expression and obtain \(ae^{ax+b}\text{.}\) This misses our integrand by a factor of \(a\text{.}\) We adjust by that factor and find the antiderivative is \(\frac{1}{a} e^{ax+b}+C\text{.}\)
