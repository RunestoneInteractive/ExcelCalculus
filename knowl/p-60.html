a) For this example we will use “designer variables”. The output will be Pay, and the input variable – the number of hours worked – will be hrs. We are told that Pay = 500 when hrs = 40. The slope of the line work beyond 40 hours is \(m=18\text{.}\) Another way to think of this is to say that there is a fixed Pay of $500 and a variable Pay for any hours in excess of 40: i.e. (hrs - 40). Thus, the equation of the line, according to the point-slope form is

\begin{equation*} Pay=variable\ pay+Fixed\ pay. \end{equation*}

\begin{equation*} Pay=m(hrs-40)+500=18*(hrs-40)+500. \end{equation*}
