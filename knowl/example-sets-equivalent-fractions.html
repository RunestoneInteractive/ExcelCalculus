Example5.2.21

Let \(p\), \(q\), \(r\), and \(s\) be integers, where \(q\) and \(s\) are nonzero. Define \(p/q \sim r/s\) if \(ps = qr\). Clearly \(\sim\) is reflexive and symmetric. To show that it is also transitive, suppose that \(p/q \sim r/s\) and \(r/s \sim t/u\), with \(q\), \(s\), and \(u\) all nonzero. Then \(ps = qr\) and \(ru = st\). Therefore, \begin{equation*}psu = qru = qst.\end{equation*} Since \(s \neq 0\), \(pu = qt\). Consequently, \(p/q \sim t/u\).
