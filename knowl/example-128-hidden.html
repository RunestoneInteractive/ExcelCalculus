Evaluate \(\int_{-2}^1 x \exp(x^2)d\)x

Solution: (Convert everything to \(u\text{.}\)) The obvious candidate for \(u\) is \(x^2\text{.}\) Then \(du=2 dx\text{.}\) For the lower endpoint, \(x=-2\) becomes \(u=4\text{.}\) For the upper endpoint \(x=1\) becomes \(u=1\text{.}\) Substituting,

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int_{-2}^1 x e^{(x^2)}dx \amp =\frac{1}{2} \int_{-2}^1 e^{(x^2)}(2x dx) \amp \quad \hbox{ (Make u and du explicit.)}\\ \amp =\frac{1}{2} \int_{1}^{4}e^u du \amp \quad \hbox{ (Do the substitution.)}\\ \amp =\left.\frac{1}{2} e^u\right|_1^{4} \amp \quad \hbox{ (Find the antiderivative.)}\\ \amp =\frac{1}{2} e^4-\frac{1}{2} e^1. \amp \quad \hbox{ (Evaluate.)}\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
