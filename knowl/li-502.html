We can write \(h(t)\) as \(f(g(t))\) where \(g(x)=2 t+5\) and \(f(x)=e^t\) We use the rules from the previous section to compute \(g'(t)=2\) and \(f'(t)=e^t\text{.}\) Composing we get \(f' (h(t))= e^(2 t+5)\text{.}\) Thus

\begin{equation*} g'(t)=f'(h(t)) h' (t)= 2 e^{2 t+5}. \end{equation*}
