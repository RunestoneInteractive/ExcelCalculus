Proof

We will prove (1) and (3) and leave the remaining results to be proven in the exercises.

(1) Observe that\begin{align*} A \cup A & = \{ x : x \in A \text{ or } x \in A \}\\ & = \{ x : x \in A \}\\ & = A\\ \end{align*}

and

\begin{align*} A \cap A & = \{ x : x \in A \text{ and } x \in A \}\\ & = \{ x : x \in A \}\\ & = A. \end{align*}

Also, \(A \setminus A = A \cap A' = \emptyset\).

(3) For sets \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\),\begin{align*} A \cup (B \cup C) & = A \cup \{ x : x \in B \text{ or } x \in C \}\\ & = \{ x : x \in A \text{ or } x \in B, \text{ or } x \in C \}\\ & = \{ x : x \in A \text{ or } x \in B \} \cup C\\ & = (A \cup B) \cup C. \end{align*}

A similar argument proves that \(A \cap (B \cap C) = (A \cap B) \cap C\).
