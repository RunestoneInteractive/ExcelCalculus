Exercise 2.

Let \(f(x) = 6 x^2 + 3\text{.}\) We are told that \(F(x) = 2 x^3 + 3 x - 2\) is an anti-derivative.

  1. Verify that \(f(x)\) is a derivative of \(F(x)\text{.}\)

  2. Use the fundamental theorem of calculus to evaluate \(\int_{-2}^4 f(x)\ dx\text{.}\)

  3. Approximate \(\int_{-2}^4 f(x)\ dx\text{,}\) using Riemann sums and 100 intervals.
