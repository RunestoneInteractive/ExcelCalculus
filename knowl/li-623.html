Item 6.2.1.3.b.

Find the partial derivatives of the original function.

\begin{equation*} f_x (x,y)=(2x)(x+2^y )+(x^2 )(1+0 ) \text{ (product rule)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 2x^2+2x 2^y+x^2=3x^2+(2x)2^y \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f_y (x,y)=\frac{\partial}{\partial y} (x^2 )(x+2^y ) \quad (\text{ Take partial derivative wrt }y.) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\frac{\partial}{\partial y}(x^3+x^2 2^y ) \qquad\text{ (Simplify the expression.)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = x^2 2^y \ln(2) \qquad (x \text{ is treated as a constant so the }x^3\text{ term drops out.)} \end{equation*}
