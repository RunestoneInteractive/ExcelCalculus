Item A.

The formula for elasticity is:

\begin{equation*} E=\frac{-d Q}{d P}*\frac{P(Q_0 )}{Q_0} \end{equation*}

I need to compute \(P(Q_0 )\) and \(\frac{-d Q}{d P}\text{.}\) Substituting into the demand function,

\begin{equation*} P(800)=20-800/100=20-8=12 \end{equation*}

To find \(\frac{d Q}{d P}\text{,}\) I recall from related rates, that \(\frac{d Q}{d P}=1/\frac{d P}{d Q}\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \frac{d Q}{d P}=1/\left(\frac{d P}{d Q}\right)=1/\left(\frac{-1}{100}\right)=-100 \end{equation*}

Thus

\begin{equation*} Elasticity= \frac{-d Q}{d P}*\frac{P(Q_0 )}{Q_0} =-(-100)*\frac{12}{800}=1.5 \end{equation*}

Since the demand is elastic when the quantity is 800, we should lower the price, causing a relatively large increase in quantity, to raise revenue.
