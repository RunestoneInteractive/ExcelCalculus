Solution 7.8.1.1.

The two curve intersect at the point of market equilibrium, \(\left({\frac{1000}{7},\frac{850}{7}}\right)\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} ProducerSurplus= \int_0^{1000/7} \left({\frac{850}{7}}\right)- \left({50+\frac{x}{2}}\right)\ dx=5102.04 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} ConsumerSurplus= \int_0^{1000/7} \left({150-\frac{x}{5}}\right)-\left({\frac{850}{7}}\right)\ dx=2040.82. \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} TotalSocialGaine=ProducerSurplus+ConsumerSurplus=7142.86 \end{equation*}
