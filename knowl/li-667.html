Item.

From the picture we conclude that the critical point is a maximum.

As an alternative we can find the discriminant.

\begin{equation*} f_{xx} (x,y)=-\frac{1}{25} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f_{xy} (x,y)=-\frac{1}{120} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f_{yy} (x,y)=-\frac{4}{25} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} D=f_{xx} (x,y)f_{yy} (x,y)-f_{xy} (x,y)^2 \gt 0 \end{equation*}

Since \(D\gt 0\) and \(f_{xx} (x,y)\lt 0\) we have a local maximum.
