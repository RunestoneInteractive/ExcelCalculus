Solution 7.8.3.1.

The two curve intersect at the point of market equilibrium, \((109.31, 33.33)\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} ProducerSurplus= \int_0^{109.31} (33.33)- \left({50(1-(0.99)^q)}\right)\ dx=1494.79 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} ConsumerSurplus= \int_0^{109.31} \left({100(0.99)^q}\right)-33.33)\ dx=2989.58. \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} TotalSocialGaine=ProducerSurplus+ConsumerSurplus=4484.37 \end{equation*}
